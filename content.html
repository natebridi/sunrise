About this site

Day:light (version 1.0) is a creation of Nate Bridi. © 2011. This site was developed as a simple web app experiment with Javascript and HTML canvas. It's still a work in progress and new features, such as the ability to compare two locations, are being developed. It is tested in the latest versions of Firefox, Chrome and Safari; support for other browsers is currently spotty. If you find a bug, or just have an opinion you'd like to share, I would love your feedback!

How times are calculated

Sunrise and sunset times on Day:light are calculated using the same algorithms employed by the NOAA. Times are accurate within one minute for +/- 72° latitude. Attempts were made to account for all sunrise/sunset times, but at extreme latitudes it may not be as reliable. Latitude, longitude, and timezones are determined by the Yahoo! PlaceFinder service. Daylight savings time is accounted for in the calculations, but if you live in one of the small regions that do not use it, you will have to adjust by one hour accordingly.
